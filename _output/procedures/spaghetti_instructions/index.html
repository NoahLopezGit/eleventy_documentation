Ingredients:

  1. 1 lb spaghetti
  2. 4 cups of water
  3. 2 tablespoons of salt
  4. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  5. 4 cloves garlic, minced
  6. 1 can (28 ounces) of crushed tomatoes
  7. 1 teaspoon dried basil
  8. 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  9. Salt and pepper to taste
  10. Freshly grated Parmesan cheese, for serving
  11. Fresh basil leaves, for serving

Step 1

 In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the salt to the boiling water. Once the water is boiling, add the spaghetti. Ensure all the pasta is completely submerged. Cook for about 8-10 minutes, or until al dente.

Step 2

 While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes. Be careful not to burn it.

Step 3

 Add the crushed tomatoes to the skillet. Stir in the dried basil and oregano. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 4

 Once the spaghetti is cooked to your liking, drain it in a colander. Do not rinse the pasta; the residual heat and starch will help the sauce adhere better.

Step 5

 Add the drained spaghetti to the skillet with the sauce. Toss until the pasta is well-coated in the sauce.

Step 6

 Divide the spaghetti among serving dishes. Top with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves. Serve hot.
